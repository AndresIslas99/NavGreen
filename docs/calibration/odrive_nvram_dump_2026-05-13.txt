  pole_pairs       = 20    (on both axes)

Therefore the CRITICAL-02-02 1.25× factor (geometric 0.0625 vs runtime
0.0781) is NOT explained by a NVRAM mis-configuration of pole_pairs or
encoder.cpr. The hypothesis tested by the dump procedure assumed fw
0.5.x and a single-axis firmware divergence; that is not the case here.

Remaining hypotheses to confirm CRITICAL-02-02:
  (H1) The M8325s motor's spec pp differs from 20. Need datasheet.
       If spec is 20 → no firmware bug, the 1.25× is mechanical.
       If spec is 25 → 20/25 = 0.8 → 1.25× effective. Likely candidate.
  (H2) Real gearbox ratio differs from the 10:1 assumed by
       agv_odrive/src/odrive_can_node.cpp and robot_geometry.yaml.
       If real gearbox is 12.5:1, 10/12.5 = 0.8 → 1.25× effective.
       Inspect the physical gearbox stamp on the motor to confirm.
  (H3) Wheel radius is actually 0.0781 m (the caliper measurement that
       reported 0.0625 m may have been the bare hub diameter, not the
       inflated tire / tread surface). Re-measure with a tape around
       the wheel circumference and divide by 2π. If C/(2π) ≈ 0.078,
       the geometric truth was wrong from the start.

The F1 1 m forward test in Section-0 Day 2 is the empirical arbiter
of which hypothesis (if any) holds.

=== Bilateral asymmetries discovered (NEW finding, not in audit) ===

Beyond the documented CRITICAL-02-02 scope, the dump reveals two
parameter mismatches between the LEFT and RIGHT controllers:

  vel_integrator_gain    LEFT 0.333    RIGHT 0.167   2× ratio
  vel_limit_tolerance    LEFT 1.30     RIGHT 1.20
  can.encoder_msg_rate   LEFT 10 ms    RIGHT 0 ms (DISABLED on RIGHT)

Implications:

(A) vel_integrator_gain asymmetry: the right wheel's controller will
    correct errors twice as slowly as the left's. On a straight-line
    command, the wheels will diverge in transient response — left
    settles faster, contributing a yaw bias whose sign depends on the
    direction of the residual error. This is plausibly part of the
    caster-drag / heading-drift signature that the iter-46 caster
    sprint tried to compensate with covariance inflation.

(B) encoder_msg_rate_ms = 0 on RIGHT: that disables automatic
    encoder updates via CAN. The right wheel encoder estimate must
    therefore be obtained via RTR polls in odrive_can_node. If at
    any point the polling stalls (CAN bus saturation, RTR loss),
    the right wheel odometry would silently freeze. Bug pattern
    similar to the original 2026-04-13 audit findings.

Section-0 verdict: capture as a NEW finding for the post-Section-0
backlog. Both asymmetries are NVRAM-resident (firmware) so the fix
is a one-time `save_configuration()` after writing matched values;
that requires a separate maintenance window and is intentionally NOT
done inside Section 0.
